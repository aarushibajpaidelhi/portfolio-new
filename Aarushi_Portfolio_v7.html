Case Study 01 · Brand Architecture · Positioning

Separating Vidyapeeth Pathshala From a ₹Bn Portfolio

PW had a cannibalisation problem — three verticals, one audience, zero separation. I was tasked with giving Vidyapeeth Pathshala a distinct identity that would hold up against India's largest offline chains while living inside PW's broader brand house.

Problem

Three PW verticals — JEE Wallah, NEET Wallah, and Vidyapeeth Pathshala — were competing for the same student. No student could articulate what Vidyapeeth Pathshala offered that the others didn't. Internal cannibalisation was eroding all three.

How I solved it
Insight

This wasn't a content problem or a media-spend problem. It was an architecture problem. The brand needed to answer one question clearly: what does offline give a student that online never can?

Framework

Mapped PW's full vertical portfolio against audience intent stages. Built separation on three pillars: a defined student identity anchored to offline value, intent-based messaging hierarchies, and strict boundary rules across all touchpoints.

Output

A brand differentiation framework: Vidyapeeth Pathshala student identity, messaging stack per persona, competitive positioning vs. Allen/Aakash/FIITJEE, and separation rules across 4 audience segments.

Impact

Brand differentiation framework adopted as-is into FY2025–26 planning. Influenced campaign messaging, content direction, and go-to-market priorities across a 198-centre vertical.

Case Study 02 · Competitive Intelligence · Brand Strategy

The Competitive Audit That Became the FY2026 Strategy

Vidyapeeth Pathshala needed to compete with Allen, Aakash, FIITJEE, and Resonance across India. Before budgets or campaigns, we needed to understand exactly what we were walking into — not anecdotally, but systematically.

Problem

No structured competitive intelligence existed. Decisions were made on perception — 'Allen is strong in Kota' — rather than verified data about how these brands actually positioned, messaged, and operated pan-India.

How I solved it
Insight

Most audits look at the obvious — pricing and ads. The real signals are in subtler choices: what language brands use with parents vs. students, how they frame success, where the white space actually lives.

Framework

Pan-India 5-dimension audit: positioning, messaging hierarchy per segment, content strategy, tonal identity, and a white space map identifying territory none of the major chains credibly owned.

Output

Competitive landscape map, white space analysis, and a positioning recommendation anchored to the insight that no major offline chain owned 'structured offline environment with digital-grade content quality.'

Impact

Adopted as-is into FY2025–26 annual strategy — not revised, not shelved. Directly set campaign and positioning direction for the year across a pan-India network.

Case Study 03 · Event Management · Brand Activation

Running PW's Highest-Sales Brand Events End to End

Vidyapeeth Pathshala's highest enrolment volumes don't come from digital campaigns — they come from 4 flagship events that create brand authority, peer social proof, and in-room urgency simultaneously.

Problem

Large-scale multi-city events with high commercial stakes require end-to-end ownership — not hand-offs between departments. The challenge: running events that felt seamless to 500+ students and parents while managing venue, logistics, faculty, outreach, branding, and follow-up simultaneously.

How I solved it
The Events

Vijay Mahotsav — community brand event, 500+ students. Vishwas Diwas — trust-building for undecided students and parents. Disha Event — guidance format, highest conversion rate. NSAT Launch — primary paid-acquisition event for new batch intake.

What I Owned

Full lifecycle per event: concept, venue, city-level logistics, student and parent outreach, on-ground branding, faculty scheduling, day-of execution, and post-event enrolment follow-up pipeline.

Scale

500+ students per flagship event. Multi-city coordination. 4+ stakeholder groups managed simultaneously — academic, ops, design, and centre leadership — with zero missed deadlines across 4 years.

Impact

500+ students per flagship event. 100–200 walk-ins per campaign cycle driven by event momentum. 4 years, 4 flagship events, zero delivery failures.

Case Study 04 · Marketing Operations · Growth Systems

Replacing Manual Research With an Automated Intelligence Pipeline

The content team needed daily visibility into what JEE and NEET students were talking about. This was being done manually — checking Reddit, YouTube, news sites. Unsystematic, slow, and always behind.

Problem

Manual trend-monitoring meant content decisions were made on yesterday's signals at best. By the time a trending topic was identified and a brief was written, the window had often passed.

How I solved it
Insight

The signal sources were all public and structured — Google News RSS, Reddit, YouTube Data API. The bottleneck wasn't access to information, it was the manual effort of aggregating and interpreting it.

The Build

n8n workflow pulling daily signals from Google News RSS, Reddit (r/JEEpreparation, r/NEETpreparation), and YouTube Data API — fed into an AI layer that generates content briefs, delivered to Gmail every morning.

Output

Daily AI-generated briefs before the team starts work. All data logged to Google Sheets for pattern tracking. Built and maintained without any engineering resource.

Impact

~5 hours of manual research replaced every week. Trend response improved from reactive (24–48h after peak) to proactive (brief in hand before peak). Zero engineering support required.
